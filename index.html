HOUSE RULES

In standard table tennis, games are officially played to 11 points. The older 21-point system was phased out in 2001, but recreational players often still use it — or a 15-point format. Regardless of the target, the game must always be won by a 2-point margin.

TO 11
OFFICIAL ITTF RULE
SERVICE
Players alternate serving 2 points at a time.
DEUCE
If the score ties at 10–10, serves alternate every 1 point until one player gains a 2-point lead (e.g. 12–10 or 15–13).
TO 15
COMMON RECREATIONAL FORMAT
SERVICE
Players alternate serving 2 or 3 points at a time (often agreed upon locally before starting).
DEUCE
If the score ties at 14–14, serves alternate every 1 point until someone wins by 2 points.
TO 21
OLD OFFICIAL RULE
SERVICE
Players alternate serving 5 points at a time.
DEUCE
If the score ties at 20–20, serves alternate every 1 point until a 2-point lead is established.
★ WHATEVER THE TARGET — WIN BY 2. ALWAYS. ★
